## Full description  (max 4000 chars — this one is ~2660)

Three drafts were written from different angles (player-fantasy / systems-depth / calm-positioning)
and scored by a three-judge panel; this is the winning skeleton with the panel's unanimous grafts applied.

```
You are the maintenance chief of a factory that should have been scrapped years ago. The machines are old, the budget is thin, and the whole line runs on your judgment. Walk the floor, listen for trouble, and keep this place alive one shift at a time.

Retrofit Factory 3D is a calm, methodical strategy game. Nothing is on fire. Nothing is rushing you. But every machine carries hidden wear, and inspection is how you find the truth — heat, vibration, drift, pressure. A fault is data, not code: read the symptoms, weigh the evidence, and find the problem before it finds you. Then make the call. Repair now, schedule it for later, or run it to failure — on purpose, because the numbers say so.

Your shift, your call
Each day you owe a contract target — fall below the minimum and the crew strikes. Before the shift starts, the planner gives you an honest production forecast, the known risks, and the line's bottleneck. Then it's yours: spend cash on spare parts, direct a repair technician who can only be in one place at a time, hire an apprentice, or gamble that the old oven has one more day in it. Days stack into weeks, weeks into a season, and every season ends with a goal.

Walk the floor
This is not a menu game. Move through the plant in full 3D with a virtual joystick, stand next to a machine, and inspect it up close. Prefer the overview? An overhead factory map supports click-to-travel so you can jump straight to the trouble.

Four plants, four kinds of trouble
• Old Bakery Plant — 6 machines, one bread contract, a 4-week season. Included from the start.
• Ironline Brewery & Bottling Works — 12 machines on steam, glycol, and air buses; fouling, calibration drift, and live hygiene to manage.
• Emberline Diecast & Alloy Works — 12 machines on gas, hydraulic, and water buses; a heat bank you must never let freeze, and a rework spiral that melts metal twice.
• Blackweir Municipal Waterworks — 12 machines on power, air, and chem buses; the river never stops and the permit never sleeps.

The three premium plants are currently open as a free preview while the store is built.

No dice, no noise
The simulation is deterministic and seeded: no hidden hand on the scales. If the line runs clean, that was you; if the plant goes down, it went down because of a call you made. No ads. No tracking or analytics. No in-app purchases right now. The game runs fully offline after the first load, with optional cloud saves — sign in with an email code or Google, or never sign in at all.

Easy on your phone
Authored 3D machines and props with ambient occlusion, bloom, and day lighting — plus a Reduced Effects mode for older phones.

Seasons end. Machines don't get younger. Clock in whenever you like — the plant will be waiting.
```
